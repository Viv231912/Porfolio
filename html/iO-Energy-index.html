iO Energy

Customer Dashboard Redesign
Creating a smooth user experience for customers

Overview

iO Energy delivers affordable renewable energy to homes and businesses. They offer consumers an effective way to reduce their energy costs with smart electricity meters and ‘time-of-use’ pricing.

Goal

The goal was to redesign the current customer portal to create a smooth user experience for iO Energy customers. This included displaying recommendations regarding the best data visualisations for different metrics, the layout of data visualisations on pages, and the overall navigation of data reports.

Team

I worked closely with 6 other classmates, since we all located in different states throughout Australia. We maintained regular check-ins to make sure our work contiuned to stay aligned.

Problem Statement

Australian energy consumers, who are cost and environmentally conscious, are unaware of how they can optimise energy usage to reduce their costs and carbon footprint as they lack relevant data or personalised insights to make smarter decisions.

Research Finding

After conducting research, it was apparent that the initial problem statement did not fully align with the findings. The problem statement was revised to better reflect the needs of the market.

Problem Statement 2.0

Australian energy consumers, who are cost and environmentally conscious, are unaware of how they can optimise energy usage to reduce their costs and carbon footprint as they lack relevant data or personalised insights to make smarter decisions.

Define

Goal

To define the target user’s needs, goals, motivations and frustrations based on research findings

Affinity Mapping

Persona

After the interviews and user testing, insights were organised into common themes in a simple and detailed maps. The affinity map revealed that almost all users tried to reduce usage, typically by using more efficient products, setting times for appliance use or installing solar energy systems. There was a combination of environmental and economic factors. Users generally picked a provider based on price, convenience, renewables or alternatively had no choice (apartment living). Users saved money by reducing consumption through provider incentives or with better plans. The portal was used primarily for billing, checking usage and viewing current prices.

Desired dashboard data included:

  • The main reasons why users used their customer portal to check their bills and current usages.
  • 49% of users were unsure whether they had a smart or basic meter on their property.
  • 90% of the users would consider switching to renewable energy.

The key themes and ideas that came out of the brainstorming sessions and created an MVP matrix to decide on what could achieve and to re-write her stories.

The solutions

The User Flow and Information Architecture was developed to ensure that the customer portal fulfilled the requirements of users as defined through the results of the research. The design of the portal is intended to be simple to use and allows decisions to be made intuitively by users.
The storyboard was also developed with two new features which iO Energy could take into consideration for phase two of the development.
