iO Energy

Customer Dashboard Redesign

Creating a smooth user experience for customers

Overview

iO Energy delivers affordable renewable energy to homes and businesses. They offer consumers an effective way to reduce their energy costs with smart electricity meters and ‘time-of-use’ pricing.

Goal

The goal was to redesign the current customer portal to create a smooth user experience for iO Energy customers. This included displaying recommendations regarding the best data visualisations for different metrics, the layout of data visualisations on pages, and the overall navigation of data reports.

Problem Statement

Australian energy consumers, who are cost and environmentally conscious, are unaware of how they can optimise energy usage to reduce their costs and carbon footprint as they lack relevant data or personalised insights to make smarter decisions.

Research Findings

After conducting research, it was apparent that the initial problem statement did not fully align with the findings. The problem statement was then revised to better reflect the needs of the market.

Define

Goal

Generate insights to define the target users' needs, goals, motivations and frustrations based on research findings.

Affinity Mapping
Empathy Mapping
Persona

After the interviews and user testing, insights were organised into common themes in simple and detailed maps. The affinity map revealed that almost all users tried to reduce usage, typically by using more efficient products, setting times for appliance use or installing solar energy systems. There was a combination of environmental and economic factors. Users generally picked a provider based on price, convenience, renewables or alternatively had no choice (apartment living). Users saved money by reducing consumption through provider incentives or with better plans. The portal was used primarily for billing, checking usage and viewing current prices.


Desired dashboard data included:

  • The main reasons why users used their customer portal were to check their bills and current usages.
  • 49% of users were unsure whether they had a smart or basic meter on their property.
  • 90% of the users would consider switching to renewable energy.

Ideation

To address the persona Olivia's key pain points, brainstorming sessions were conducted to create "How might we?"" statements, using techniques such as Crazy 8s. These assisted to work through Olivia's pain points. The key themes and ideas that came out of the brainstorming sessions were then used to generate an MVP matrix to identify the most important issues and ensure that Olivia's needs would be met.

The solutions

The User Flow and Information Architecture was developed to ensure that the customer portal fulfilled the requirements of users as defined through the results of the research. The design of the portal is intended to be simple to use and allows decisions to be made intuitively by users.
The storyboard was also developed with two new features which iO Energy could take into consideration for phase two of the development.

Customer Portal Uplift

The goal was improve user experience of the current customer portal, whilst aligning with the current iO Energy style guide.


The focus areas based on key pain points from user insights were:

  • Bill Comparison tool.
  • A 'lifestyle' quiz to help make it easier to determine which plan suited best. Also based on current usage and recommend best value plan.
  • Refinement of the bill comparison tool
  • Add renewables energy panel for customers who has solar panels installed.

The first wireframes were tested with 3 participants who gave a 7/10 for ease of use.

they felt it was difficult to understand the energy usage chart

would like to see a clearer indicator for the login page and menu bar

Using this feedback, the design was iterated and imagery was added within a larger panel. Further additions included modals for bill comparison and best recommended plan.

Billing Page Features

The billing page was created with the intention to only display billing information, and not cross over with usage information too much.

It shows general information such as current charges, outstanding payments and cost goals for the next bill if the user has set one.

Users will also be able to see their billing history, as well as being able to email or download the selected bill id desired.

In the comparison modal, the user will be able to compare any bills by selecting either a specific date, season, or billing period. From that, they will then be able to see the differences side by side.

Plan Recommendations

From initial research, it was found that users are still looking on comparison websites to see whether they are getting the best deal.


Users wanted to see recommendations of best value plan and the money they can save.


The plan recommendation panel was added to continuously monitor customers' usage and recommend best energy plans available.
