MyCalendar

Re-design of MyCalender App

Improve the experience of users whilst interacting with the app.

Overview

Period tracking apps enable women to understand and follow how their bodies are working. If a user has specific personal goals (such as trying to conceive for childbirth), tracking apps can be extremely useful for determining critical timeframes such as the ovulation window. Existing tracking apps have a number of disadvantages that affect their useability.

Goal

The goal of this project was to identify the issues with the MyCalendar app and provide effective solutions to improve the experience of users whilst interacting with the app.

Problem Statement

Users of the existing MyCalendar app are frustrated because they cannot manage their personal information and they also cannot personalise their experience.

Research Findings

It was clear that people from different age groups tended to have different expectations of period tracking apps, with the key findings being:

  • Users in a younger age bracket (typically under 30) were mostly interested in features related to avoiding pregnancy
  • Users between 30-40 were mostly interested in tracking their cycles to maximise their chances of conceiving
  • Users aged above 40 were mostly interested in using the app to provide data that would assist future health/medical consultations

The general consensus amongst the majority of people was that:

  • Their previous experience with tracking apps was that the app interface could be complicated which made the app challenging and frustrating to us
  • Although a range of different apps are available in the market, there was no single app that catered to all of their needs

Define

Goal

Affinity Mapping

Persona

The data collected from the research was grouped using affinity mapping which allowed a number of key themes to be identified. From the grouping, key insights into the needs of different types of users could be better understood.
In conjunction with affinity mapping and personas, empathy mapping was utilised to gain a better understanding of the needs, observations and actions of users of the MyCalendar app. The insights under “Pains” and “Gains” were especially useful in guiding the direction of the future design work.


Pains

  • Inaccurate cycle predictions
  • Data lost during the transition to new phone
  • Constant pop-up ads

Gains

  • Want the accuracy of the app to improved.
  • Want to be able to backup data and access it in the future.
  • Be able to use app with any device.
  • Wish to have a personalize option

Ideation

To address the primary persona Emily's key pain points, brainstorming sessions were conducted to create "How might we?" statements, using techniques such as Crazy 8s and storyboard. These assisted to work through Emily's pain points. The key themes and ideas that came out of the brainstorming sessions were then used to generate an MVP matrix to identify the most important issues and ensure that Emily's needs would be met.

I worked closely usability test participants throughout the whole design sprint process. I was able to design and test various prototypes with participants. Various changes were made based on learnings throughout the research process and constant validation with participants.

Task 1: Onboarding

Aim: Complete onboarding questions and create an account.

Results:

Area: sequence of the onboarding page

Feedback:

Participants found that the sequence of the onboarding page was slightly confusing and preferred to have the personal goals page at the beginning of the onboarding part.

Outcome:

The personal goals page was moved to the beginning in the final prototype.

Area: easy sign up options

Feedback:

Participants noted that they would like to have quick and easy sign up options, rather than having to go through all of the questionnaire pages.

Outcome:

Facebook and Google sign up options were added in the final prototpe. Additionally, a “skip” button was placed on each questionnaire page to provide users the option to bypass the questionnaire and therefore gain instant access.

Task 2: homepage

Aim: The app is currently showing the incorrect period day. Please update this information and enter the correct day.

Results:

Area: complex homepage

Feedback:

Participants found that there was too much information displayed on the homepage.

Outcome:

The homepage was re-designed to make it simpler to navigate and more intuitive for users.

Area: edit option

Feedback:

Participants found it difficult to find the “edit” option.

Outcome:

An “edit” button was added to the homepage. Design and placement was carefully considered to ensure that users will be able to find it easily.

Area: confirmation prompt

Feedback:

Participants suggested that it would be helpful to have a notification prompt to confirm the period start day.

Outcome:

A confirmation prompt was added to ensure that users are notified that the information has been updated in the app.

Task 3: Community

Aim: Go through the community groups and select one to follow.

Results

Feedback:

Participants found that it was easy to locate the “community” button and navigate groups. Participants suggested that they would like to have a “share” option so that users will be able to share interesting topics and articles with their friends. Participants also suggested that it would be good to have a “bookmark” option to make it easier to locate interesting items in the future.

Outcome:

“Share” and “boomark” options were implemented in the final prototype.

Task 4: Settings

Aim: Backup tracked data and export to your doctor for your next appointment.

Results

Feedback:

Participants found that the process of backing up files and transferring data was relatively easy. However, participants noted that the backup locations were not clear.

Outcome:

A prompt for selection of backup locations and a confirmation button have been added to the final prototype. The prompt appears before completion of backup.

Conclusion

This project was implemented to address issues with the existing MyCalendar app that have caused frustration to users.

The research gave a valuable insight into these concerns from the perspective of users. It provided the basis for the future design changes, responding to difficulties with the existing architecture of the app as well as addition of new functions and features.

The usability testing was extremely useful and enable the prototype to be refined, ensuring that the goals of the project were realised.

As a result of the work undertaken, the MyCalendar app now has improved functionality and will better meet the needs of users.
